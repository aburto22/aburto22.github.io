Solo Trips

by Ale

Camping near Lake Nahuel-Huapi at the end of La Ruta de los 7 Lagos, Argentina.

It all started in 2014, when I was studying in France.

When friend told me his great idea of cycling around the UK, I immediately jumped on board. We were supposed to take 2 months off between the end of our classes and the beginning of our internship. The plan was to cycle from south England to Scotland, before heading to Ireland.

Unfortunately, 3 months before our scheduled departure date, our university told us that they wouldn't allow us to leave due to insurance reasons, and that we were required to start our internship pretty much as soon as possible after finishing our classes. I was left with a bit of gear I was planning to use during my trip (a good saddle, panniers and some clothing), and unsure about what to do next.

When I finally found an internship, I realised I had a 2-week window to get up to something, which seemed to be fine with the university. 2 weeks seemed too short to cycle around the UK, and as I was living in Nantes, I decided to cycle along the Loire River. I would be heading West until Orleans, around 400km from Nantes. This would give me plenty of time to get back home and prepare for the start of the internship.

Sunset in El Valle del Elqui, Chile.

I started cycling on a grey day, with light rain showers coming and going during the whole day. It didn't matter, I was off and happy to be on the road. Unfortunately, after riding around 90km, I had to grab a train back home as I had punctured 3 times my front tyre, and needed to take a closer look to what was causing the issue. Anyway, after getting a new tyre and getting everything up and ready, I was off again.

This first bike trip, even if it was a short one, was a bit of a revelation. I truly enjoyed being on the bike all day, seeing the world at a slower pace, but fast enough to get from one city to another. It seemed the perfect balance between speed and how much load I could carry. After living in a big city almost my entire life, the peacefulness and quietness I experienced was the best part of the trip, together with the amazing sights along the way.

Guanacos in Parque Patagonia just off La Carretera Australia, Chile.

Back in Chile in 2015, I was decided to start travelling around my country as well. With Nico, a good friend, we rode together through La Ruta de los 7 Lagos, in Argentina, early 2015, our first fully self-supported trip. Then, it was El Valle del Elqui, in Chile, mid 2015. And early 2016, we headed towards La Carretera Austral, our longest and most remote trip yet. All those trips took us through mountains and forest, close to glaciers, over rivers and to the sea. We rode on steep roads, over bad gravel, and pushed our bikes many times. We were tired, sometimes grumpy, but most of the time just happy, happy to be moving, to discover and to enjoy. We met incredible people and cyclists along the way, but the best part was to be able to wake up in cold mornings, head out of the tent, grab a cup of coffee and look at the mountains, surrounded by nature.

If want to read more about these particular trips to can head to our blog sections where you can find articles about every trip.
